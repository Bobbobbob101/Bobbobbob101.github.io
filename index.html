Astrophysics Research

Aurora Borealis

I have been working with Dr. Gerard Fasel at Pepperdine University in Malibu, CA to research the sun’s interaction with earth’s magnetic field. We observe the aurora borealis from a color all sky camera and then look at interplanetary magnetic field (IMF) data using a satellite from NASA. Thus, we are able to gain a better understanding of the coupling processes between the solar wind and the Earth’s magnetosphere.

Research Experiences for Undergraduates (REU) Program

Solar Prominences

Working with research professor Dr. Vasyl Yurchyshyn at the Big Bear Solar Observatory (BBSO) in Big Bear Lake, CA, I observed solar prominences from data collected over a period of six years (2009-15). I then wrote a Python program using Numpy and Matplotlib to track the number of solar prominence per day, which I then compared to the average number of sunspots. After plotting the amount of prominences and sunspots overtime using Matplotlib, I compared my graph to one in a paper published by Dr. Navin Chandra Joshi, seeing a similar pattern and concluded that sunspots and prominences were not correlated. I presented my findings at the New Jersey Institute of Technology (NJIT) in Newark, NJ.
